**Contrarian pass:** 

1. *On agentic AI*: The TechCrunch piece "How VCs and founders use inflated ARR to crown AI startups" is the tell — if the trade press is already writing the debunking piece, the peak of the narrative cycle may be closer than founders think. Hark's $700M Series A for a "secretive universal AI interface" with no public product is a classic late-cycle signal. The companies that will win are not the ones with the best agent demos but the ones with the stickiest proprietary data loops — and most current European agentic plays have not yet demonstrated that.

2. *On EU regulation as moat*: The eu-startups.com editorial argues red tape is a moat, and Sifted's sovereignty debate echoes this. But this is only true if EU enforcement is consistent and US competitors are actually blocked — neither is guaranteed. GDPR has been on the books since 2018 and US hyperscalers still dominate European enterprise software. The moat thesis requires believing that AI Act enforcement will be materially stricter than GDPR enforcement, which is a high bar.

3. *On quantum*: The Sifted "SPACs and megarounds" framing for quantum is a warning sign — SPACs historically mark the top of a hype cycle (see: space tech 2021). Algorithmiq relocating to Italy for a €18M raise may reflect a funding environment that is tighter than headlines suggest, not a vote of confidence in Italian tech infrastructure.
