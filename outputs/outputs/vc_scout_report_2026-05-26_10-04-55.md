## Contrarian View
**The consensus is that European AI startups are finally "having their moment" and that regulatory compliance is becoming a moat.** Every European source (EU-Startups, Sifted, tech.eu) is running some version of this narrative this week.

What would have to be true for this consensus to be wrong?

1. **The moat is illusory if enforcement stays weak.** The AI Act and DORA are only moats if they are actually enforced against non-compliant US competitors. If the EU continues its pattern of passing regulation and under-enforcing it (see: GDPR fines vs. actual behavioral change at Big Tech), European compliance-native startups bear the cost of compliance without the competitive benefit.

2. **The ARR inflation problem is not US-only.** TechCrunch's warning about inflated ARR metrics applies directly to European AI SaaS rounds being celebrated this week. If Lucis, Scope, or Harmoney are using loose ARR definitions (committed ARR, pipeline ARR, etc.), their Series A valuations may be built on the same sand as the US companies being criticized.

3. **The quantum hype cycle may be peaking, not starting.** Sifted's own framing — "SPACs and megarounds: What's going on in European quantum?" — implies the market is already asking whether this is a bubble. Algorithmiq relocating to Italy for a €18M raise could signal that the easy Nordic/UK quantum money has dried up, not that Italy is a new quantum hub.

4. **General Catalyst's European push is a US fund deploying at US valuations.** Their backing of Lucis may be pricing out European follow-on investors at Series B, not validating the European ecosystem.
